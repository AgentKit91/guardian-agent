The reality of boutique agency life

Smaller agencies are
left to figure it out.

Nobody sits you down and teaches you how to read a production contract. You learn on the job, make calls based on gut feeling, and hope you haven't missed anything important.

01
No one trains you in contracts

Boutique agencies are often run by one or two people who built their expertise in talent, relationships, and instinct. Not contract law. There's no induction, no legal handbook, no colleague to ask.

02
No team of lawyers or negotiators

The big agencies have entire departments. You have yourself. The same person who takes client calls, chases availability, and submits to casting is also expected to read, understand, and respond to contracts across every medium and deal type.

03
Professional advice is expensive

Entertainment lawyers charge hundreds per hour. For a boutique agency running on tight margins, instructing a lawyer for every contract simply is not realistic. So most agencies carry the risk alone.

04
Generic tools don't speak your language

Off-the-shelf contract tools don't know what a CAN is. They don't understand BBC repeat structures, commercial buyouts, or favoured nations clauses. You need something built for entertainment.

05
A missed clause can cost your client

An overbroad exclusivity window. A repeat fee below benchmark. An image rights clause with no expiry. Each one chips away at your client's career and your agency's reputation.
